[I moved to Rotterdam](/now/#2025-09-23), The Netherlands, on the <time datetime=2025-08-25>25<sup>th</sup> August 2025</time>. I will be living in this city and this country for at least two years, as I am [studying at XPUB](/now/#2025-09-23). Here are my notes about life in this place.

## Bureaucracy

Most, virtually all, the legal procedures in the Netherlands hinge on being registered, hence having a <q>BSN</q>. Here are all the seps I had to go through to be fully settled in, chronologically sorted.

1. [University registration and certificates](#University)
2. [Registration](https://www.rotterdam.nl/en/first-registration-in-the-netherlands/start-registration-in-the-netherlands-by-a-student) at the municipality (<em lang=nl>gemeente</em>)
3. Opening a Netherlandian [bank account](#Bank%20account)
4. Even if it is possible to access and pay for all public transport in the whole country via credit card, it is cheaper and more convenient to get an [OV-chipkaart](https://www.ov-chipkaart.nl/). It requires a Dutch bank account.
5. Pay for [health insurance](Healthcare%20in%20the%20Netherlands.md), and get a <abbr title='General Practitioner'>GP</abbr>.
