[I moved to Rotterdam](/now/#2025-09-23), Netherlands, on the <time datetime=2025-08-25>25<sup>th</sup> August 2025</time>. I will be living in this city and this country for at least two years, as I am [studying at XPUB](/now/#2025-09-23). Here are my notes about life in this place.

## Bureaucracy

Most, virtually all, the legal procedures in the Netherlands hinge on being registered, hence having a <q>BSN</q>. Here are all the seps I had to go through to be fully settled in, chronologically sorted.

1. [University registration and certificates](#University)
2. Registration
	1. [Briefadres](https://www.rotterdam.nl/briefadres-aanvragen), via [post](https://www.rotterdam.nl/briefadres-aanvragen/start-briefadres-aanvragen-per-post)
	2. Final registration
3. Bank account at [Triodos Bank](https://www.triodos.nl/)
4. Even if it is possible to access and pay for all public transport in the whole country via credit card, it is cheaper and more convenient to get an [OV-chipkaart](https://www.ov-chipkaart.nl/). It requires a Dutch bank account, but is does not have to be the same as the OV-chipkaart’s owner.
5. Get a general practitioner

## Biobulkbende

I joined [Biobulkbende](https://biobulkbende.org/en) food cooperative in October 2025. It is great to buy organic food in a sustainable way, prioritizing what is produced locally, and fostering direct connections with producers, even though food is mainly bought via [Odin](https://www.odin.nl/).

The only requirement to be a member of Biobulkbende is to contribute to the cooperative by volunteering ≥2 hours per month in a working group. I decided to join the <q>Shop</q> group, meaning that on every Monday evening I make sure to be at the collection spot, and help people pick up what they ordered through [Foodsoft](https://foodcoops.net/), our self-hosted coordination software, made purposefully for food coops.
