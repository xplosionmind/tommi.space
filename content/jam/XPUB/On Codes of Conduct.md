## Personal considerations

As we discuss the creation of a code of conduct for XPUB, I finally begin to think about its importance and its value beyond its mere enforcement.

I have always been extremely lazy and I approached any kind of human interaction or conflict in a very bespoke way. Probably also because of my privilege, I have never had any direct experience of CoC violation/enforcement, and I wrongly underestimated the significance of a code of conduct, even though rationally I have never questioned the relevance and the necessity of having one.

Actually, this is even worse considering that I am the administrator and moderator of a Fediverse instance, even though tiny. All the reports/incidents I had to deal with in the ~3 years of its existence have always been related to spam, thus with very simple and clear-cut resolutions. Nevertheless, I have always known that our server rules are quite broad and limited. I quickly drafted them when I was setting up the server for the first time, and never revisited them. This is particularly negative especially because I believe it is of paramount importance to revise and possibly amend it in a collective dimension, with Pan members, not alone.

## Why?

Even though it is kind of self-evident why a code of conduct would be necessary, there are some more nuanced and deeper reasons that make its creation ever more valuable.

As I also noted in the [XPUB’s Code of Conduct Wiki Discussion page](https://pzwiki.wdka.nl/mediadesign/Talk:Code_of_Conduct 'Talk:Code of Conduct – XPUB & Lens-Based wiki'):

Why do we *need* a Code of Conduct? What are the shortcomings of the [WdKA Code of Conduct](https://one.mywdka.nl/organisation/code-of-conduct/ '(private URL)') that we fall under by default?

- Establishing a sense of community, and highlighting the values of the XPUB community
- From Jo Freeman’s <cite>[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Tyranny_of_Structurelessness The Tyranny of Structurelessness]</cite>: if (power) dynamics and structures are not may clear, spontaneous and potentially unfair defaults might emerge.
- Relationship with other organizations’ and institutions’ codes of conduct. For instance: what if XPUB is hosting an event in a space that has its own code of conduct (e.g. WORM)?
- Making it explicit and concrete what is considered as harassment, so that a further layer of discrimination is removed when a person steps up with an experience of harassment?
- <q>For me, Codes of Conduct were part of a feminist project that confronts systemic oppression through the work of articulation</q> ~ Femke Snelting – <cite>[Codes of Conduct](https://monoskop.org/images/d/de/Sollfrank_Cornelia_The_Beautiful_Warriors_Technofeminist_Praxis_in_the_21st_Century_2019.pdf#page=64)</cite>
