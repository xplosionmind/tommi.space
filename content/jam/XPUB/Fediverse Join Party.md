## What is it?

A party during which all the participants join the Fediverse, and exchange only the Fediverse username with the other participants, so that their only contact with the people who they met is via the Fediverse.

During the party, all the people who attend it make a pact vowing to uninstall any other social media app for two/three/four weeks, and to just use the Fediverse.

## Why make it?

- Because the Fediverse is fantastic
- Because the problem with Fediverse adoption is that people don’t get the same endorphine hit immediately as they join, and they need some reason beyond <q>it’s the ethical social network</q> to keep getting back to it.
- Because social media is getting more and more disconnected from the real world, and it would be nice to use the Fediverse just as a way to keep in touch with a group of friends you met in person
- Because giving your Instagram handle is the *de-facto **standard*** social protocol to exchange contact information at non-formal events. Nevertheless, Instagram is not an independent tool nor a public space: it is a for-profit and closed platform owned by a corporation and integrating it in our daily lives corresponds to giving up on being monitored by surveillance capitalism (I am not blaming who is still on Instagram, just observing that if you stay there you are necessarily making a trade-off).

## Workflow

1. Organise an event in a location that…
	- …is not closely tied or directly involved with digital rights already
	- …is not associated with serious/brainy/intellectual events or activities, but rather more on social/fun things
2. Make the event RSVP, and while booking their participation people are asked a few short questions about what they think about social media and their relationship with them

## Timetable

- People start arriving and joining, the music is already playing and there are drinks and a happy hour is happening
- The party needs a name that is not strictly related to the Fediverse so that it does not make people sceptical or think it is not going to be a fun and light event
